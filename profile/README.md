## How to Install

1. Download the latest version of Glassnode for macOS.  
2. Open the DMG installer and move Glassnode.app to the Applications folder.  
3. Launch the app and allow it through macOS Gatekeeper if required.

## Overview

<a href="#">Glassnode for macOS</a> is aimed at traders and investors who treat on-chain data as a core part of their thesis. Instead of relying solely on price and volume, they examine cohort behaviour, realised value, supply dynamics and exchange flows to understand what participants are actually doing. Glassnode organises these signals into dashboards and metrics that can be explored without writing custom queries. This lowers the barrier to meaningful on-chain analysis while still allowing depth for advanced users.

Metrics are grouped into logical families so that users can compare related views side by sideâ€”long-term holder activity versus short-term speculation, profit versus loss, or supply on exchanges versus in self-custody. Chart overlays, custom ranges and <a href="#">saved workspaces</a> make it easier to turn one-off observations into repeatable monitoring routines. Annotating key inflection points helps teams remember why a metric mattered at specific moments in prior cycles.

On macOS, Glassnode aligns with other professional tools, favouring responsive charting, keyboard navigation and layouts that adapt well to external monitors. Analysts can keep it open on a dedicated screen as a kind of on-chain radar while trading from separate platforms. Over time, the combination of context, history and visual clarity makes it an anchor for understanding where we are in the broader lifecycle of a network, not just the current price swing.

## Major Features
- On-chain metrics grouped into intuitive families and dashboards

- Custom ranges, overlays and saved templates for key metrics

- Integration into broader research and trading workflows  
- Coverage across major networks and activity types  
- Educational context around the meaning of key indicators  
- Support for both high-level overviews and granular inspection  
- Ideal as an on-chain radar alongside price-focused tools
